Struggling to make minimum payments?

Dealing with credit card debt can be challenging, especially if you're making only minimum payments.

Let's consider an example:

Say you have $15,000 in credit card debt with an average interest rate of 20%. If you stick to minimum payments (usually around 3% of the total balance), it could take you approximately 25 years to clear the debt, and you'd end up paying around $22,000 in interest alone. That means your $15,000 debt could swell to $37,000 over time.

If you're facing difficulties meeting your payments, there are options beyond bankruptcy worth exploring.
